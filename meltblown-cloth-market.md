<p><p>Meltblown cloth is a nonwoven fabric produced from thermoplastic polymers through a process that involves melting the polymer and forcing it through fine nozzles. This technique creates a web of microfibers that are highly absorbent, lightweight, and offer excellent barrier properties, making meltblown cloth an essential material in various applications, particularly in the healthcare and hygiene sectors. Its primary use includes filtration, medical masks, and protective clothing, which gained significant attention due to the COVID-19 pandemic.</p><p>The Meltblown Cloth Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 10.9% during the forecast period. Additionally, the rising awareness of personal hygiene and the growing need for effective personal protective equipment (PPE) are further fueling the market. Recent trends indicate a shift towards sustainable and recyclable meltblown materials, as manufacturers aim to address environmental concerns.
