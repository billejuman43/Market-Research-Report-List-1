<p><p>The melamine doors market primarily features two core types: MDF (Medium Density Fiberboard) core and particleboard core. MDF core doors offer a smooth, dense surface that resists warping and provides excellent durability and finish quality, making them suitable for high-end applications. In contrast, particleboard core doors are typically more cost-effective, using pressed wood particles for their structure; they are lightweight and offer adequate performance for residential use. Both options are popular for their decorative finishes and versatility in interior design.</p></p>
