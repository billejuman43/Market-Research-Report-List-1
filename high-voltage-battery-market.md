<p><p>The high voltage battery market is primarily categorized into NCA (Nickel Cobalt Aluminum), NMC (Nickel Manganese Cobalt), LFP (Lithium Iron Phosphate), and Others. NCA batteries emphasize high energy density, making them suitable for electric vehicles. NMC batteries balance energy density, cost, and stability, often used in a range of applications, including EVs and grid storage. LFP batteries are known for their safety and thermal stability but lower energy density, popular in electric buses. Others include emerging technologies like solid-state batteries or lithium-sulfur variations.</p></p>
