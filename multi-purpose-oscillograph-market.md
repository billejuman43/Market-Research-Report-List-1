<p><p>The multi-purpose oscillograph market encompasses two primary types: digital oscilloscopes and analog oscilloscopes. Digital oscilloscopes convert signal data into digital format, allowing for advanced features such as complex calculations, data storage, and waveform analysis, making them ideal for modern electronic diagnostics. Analog oscilloscopes, on the other hand, display waveforms in real-time on a cathode-ray tube, offering simplicity and immediate visual insight, which is beneficial for educational purposes and basic troubleshooting tasks. Both types serve essential roles in testing and measurement.</p></p>
